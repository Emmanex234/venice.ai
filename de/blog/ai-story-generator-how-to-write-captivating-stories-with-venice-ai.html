AI story generator: How to write captivating stories with Venice.ai

Venice.ai

Artificial intelligence is revolutionizing creative writing. AI story generators allow anyone to quickly produce engaging narratives, from short stories to novel outlines. Venice.ai offers a powerful, privacy-focused platform for crafting AI-generated stories without compromising your creative freedom.

What is Venice.ai?

Venice.ai is a free, uncensored AI story generator that respects your privacy. Unlike mainstream AI writing tools, Venice doesn't store your prompts or generated content. 

This gives you full control over your creative process while leveraging cutting-edge AI technology.

How to use an AI story writer

To create compelling stories with Venice.ai, incorporate these key elements into your prompts:

  • Genre: Specify the type of story you want (e.g., science fiction, romance, mystery)
  • Story length: Indicate whether you want a short story, novella, or full-length novel
  • Plot: Outline the main events or conflicts driving your story
  • Narrative perspective: Choose first-person, third-person limited, or omniscient narration
  • Character names and descriptions: Provide details about your main characters
  • Setting description: Paint a picture of where and when your story takes place

Tip: To help guide Venice, first write a detailed prompt asking Venice.ai to outline the story. Then, manually edit the outline to your liking and provide the final outline back to Venice.ai to write the longer story.

Using system prompts to help write stories with AI in Venice.ai

Venice.ai allows you to customize the AI's behavior using system prompts. This feature lets you tailor the writing style to your preferences. 

For example:

"You are a novelist who writes in the style of Ernest Hemingway. Use concise, straightforward, and realistic sentences to engage the reader throughout the story. Focus on vivid sensory details and sparse dialogue to create a strong sense of atmosphere and character."

Open settings by clicking the gear in the prompt field, and add your system prompt in the settings menu.

You can save multiple system prompts and toggle a system prompt to “active” when you want to use it for a project. Be sure to remember to toggle it off when you don’t intend to use it.

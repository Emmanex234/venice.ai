AI research tools: Venice vs ChatGPT and Claude

Venice offers a powerful tool: a private, uncensored AI that combines web search capabilities with in-depth document analysis that can be used as an AI research assistant.

Venice doesn’t censor or spy on your AI research

Unlike mainstream AI tools that censor content and store your data, Venice respects your intellectual freedom and privacy. We don't impose content restrictions beyond those inherent in the open-source models we offer. Your prompts, conversations, and uploaded documents are stored locally in your browser only, ensuring your research remains private.

If you use ChatGPT or Claude, on the other hand, all your documents are stored forever on their servers, and may be leaked, hacked, subpoenaed or spied on by staff or outside parties. Moreover, Claude does not have search capabilities, so the information will be outdated and limited to its training data.

But our privacy-first approach offers significant benefits beyond just confidentiality. When your AI research isn't stored and tracked, you won't be bombarded with targeted ads related to your queries.

Which AI platform is best in accuracy for research?

Mainstream AI platforms censor output according to content restriction, resulting in withholding information and less accurate research.

The absence of censorship and tracking on Venice.ai allows for more objective, accurate research compared to mainstream search engines, or AI such as ChatGPT and Claude. Your past searches won't bias the information you receive, ensuring a fresh perspective on every topic. Privacy is particularly valuable when investigating controversial or sensitive topics, as it allows for unbiased exploration without fear of repercussion or judgment, whether now or in the future.

Bottom line, Venice’s research capabilities empower you to:

  • Get the latest accurate and up-to-date information on any topic using AI web searches
  • Dive into controversial subjects without bias,censorship, or future repercussion
  • Analyze complex documents and cross-reference with web data
  • Be free from targeted ads and tracking based on your research queries
  • Avoid breaching confidentiality of your materials

Whether you're an academic researcher pushing the boundaries of knowledge, a business analyst seeking market insights, or a curious individual exploring complex or controversial topics, Venice provides the tools you need without compromising on privacy or freedom of inquiry.

Using Venice for effective AI web searches 

Venice's web search model gives you access to the latest information on the internet to fuel your research. Here's how to use this powerful tool effectively.

Step 1: Select our web-enabled model

Open Venice and click the settings button in the chat input field. 

In the Settings menu under Selected Model, choose "Nous Theta 8B (Fastest & Web-enabled)" to activate Venice’s internet search capabilities.

Step 2: Craft and submit your research prompt

Frame your question as a specific research task, not as a simple question. 

Instead of "Tell me about AI in finance," try something like "Research and summarize the 3 major trends and developments in the application of AI in finance over the past year. Cite recent sources and studies." 

Type your prompt into the chat input field and hit Enter.

Step 3: Review the response

Read through Venice's response, looking for key points and insights. Pay attention to any sources cited in the text. 

Always check these sources – you can click on or copy the links at the end of the response to verify the information independently.

Step 4: Ask follow-up questions (aka iterative prompting)

Based on the initial results, ask more specific questions to explore further. 

For example: "Provide more details on the AI-Powered Trading Strategies you mentioned."

In-depth AI document analysis with Venice

Venice's document analysis capabilities allow you to extract insights from complex texts quickly and efficiently. 

To dig deeper into the topic of AI in Finance, let’s select one of the cited sources in the chat from the previous web search section, a study titled “Artificial intelligence in Finance: a comprehensive review through bibliometric and content analysis.”

💡 Note: Using the Llama 31. 405B model, you can upload a document with a maximum of 250K characters (about 35K words). Venice does not store your uploaded document, it’s stored locally  in your browser

Step 1: Upload your document

Begin by selecting the Llama 3.1 405B (Most Intelligent) model, then add the document to Venice.

Click the paperclip icon beside the chat input field, then select your PDF. Alternatively, you can easily drag and drop your PDF in the input field.

Venice will process the document, making its contents available for analysis.

Step 2: Analyze the document structure

Now, let's get an overview of the paper's organization.

Try asking: "How does this paper structure its analysis of AI in Finance?"

Venice will provide a concise summary of the document's main sections and their key points, giving you a bird's-eye view of the authors' approach to the topic.

Step 3: Extract specific information

Diving deeper, we can pinpoint particular details within the text.

For instance, prompt Venice with: "What AI technologies are currently being applied in the financial sector, according to this review?"

The AI will comb through the document, presenting you with a comprehensive list of AI technologies and their financial applications as discussed by the authors.

Step 4: Synthesize your findings

To grasp the paper's main conclusions, we'll need to consolidate information from various sections.

Prompt Venice: "Summarize how AI is impacting financial markets and trading, based on this paper's findings."

Venice will analyze relevant portions of the text, distilling the authors' key observations into a coherent summary of AI's effects on finance and trading.

Step 5: Identify future trends

Let's explore what the future might hold for AI in finance.

Ask Venice: "According to this paper, what trends can we expect in AI applications for banking and financial services?"

The AI will extract and synthesize information from the document's forward-looking sections, offering you a glimpse into potential future developments in the field.

Step 6: Ask follow-up questions

Finally, we can use the paper's insights to spark further inquiry.

Prompt: "Based on this review, what five critical questions should financial institutions consider when implementing AI technologies?"

Drawing from the challenges and opportunities discussed in the document, Venice will formulate thought-provoking questions to guide further exploration of AI in finance.

Getting the most out of Venice as your AI research assistant

While Venice is a powerful tool for AI research and analysis, it's crucial to use it responsibly and maintain the integrity of your work. 

Consider the following tips when using Venice for your professional research:

  • Use system prompts: Set up a system prompt containing specifics about what kind of researcher you want Venice to be: academic, business analyst, medical, etc. You can also include instructions on which research methods to employ, and how to present the results.
  • Verify sources: Always check the sources that Venice cites in its web searches. Ask Venice to provide sources for the information it gives you, then independently verify their credibility and relevance to your context.
  • Cross-reference information: Don't rely on a single source or query. Use multiple prompts to approach your problem from different angles, ensuring a comprehensive understanding of the issue.
  • Consider AI limitations: Venice, like all AI, can have limitations or biases. Though we carefully select our models for best performance, regularly ask about the scope of its knowledge and be aware of potential gaps in its understanding of your specific industry or market.
  • Think critically: Don't accept Venice's outputs at face value. Always apply your own business acumen and industry expertise to the information provided, considering its relevance and applicability to your specific situation.

Venice is designed to augment your work, not replace your expertise and judgment. Use it as a powerful assistant in your research toolkit, but always rely on your own analysis and industry knowledge for final decisions and strategies.
